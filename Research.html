Medical AI and Healthcare IoT

Integrity-Aware Wound Classification Under Image Degradation

Undergraduate Research Assistant, NH-INBRE
Keene State College
Faculty Mentor: Dr. Wei Lu
May 2026–Present

This research investigates how image-quality problems affect the reliability of machine-learning models used for wound classification. The project evaluates a MobileNetV3-Small convolutional neural network under clean and degraded image conditions.

  • Developed preprocessing and controlled image-degradation pipelines.
  • Evaluated model robustness under blur, Gaussian noise, JPEG compression, brightness loss, and reduced image quality.
  • Analyzed accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, confidence, and model failure patterns.
  • Examined the importance of image integrity in remote healthcare and connected medical systems.
  • Contributed to experimental design, interpretation, visualization, and manuscript preparation.
Key result: The model achieved 86.05% accuracy on original test images. Brightness loss produced the largest performance decline, reducing accuracy to 73.26%.

Manuscript: “Integrity-Aware Wound Classification Under Image Degradation in Healthcare IoT Systems,” submitted to WIDECOM 2026.

AI and Quantitative Modeling

A Task-Based Quantitative Model for Predicting Occupational Exposure to Artificial Intelligence

Undergraduate Researcher
Keene State College
Faculty Mentor: Dr. Wei Lu
March 2026–Present

This project develops a quantitative framework for estimating how occupations may be affected by artificial intelligence and automation. Instead of assigning one automation score to an entire occupation, the model examines the individual tasks that workers perform.

  • Cleaned, integrated, and validated occupational and task-level workforce datasets.
  • Selected variables related to work activities, technology, skills, and occupational characteristics.
  • Developed task-based measures of AI and automation exposure.
  • Evaluated patterns across occupations and job categories.
  • Co-authored the paper and contributed to the conference presentation.
Research contribution: The task-based framework provides a more detailed view of occupational exposure by recognizing that jobs contain a mixture of automatable, augmentable, and human-centered tasks.

Conference presentation: International Conference on Complex, Intelligent, and Software Intensive Systems, CISIS 2026.

Embedded Systems and Cybersecurity

Smart Syringe Pump Design

Undergraduate Researcher
Keene State College
February 2025

This project involved designing an embedded control system for automated fluid delivery using Arduino hardware and C++. The project combined control logic, hardware integration, system testing, reliability analysis, and cybersecurity.

  • Designed and programmed an Arduino-based fluid-delivery control system.
  • Integrated mechanical, electronic, and software components.
  • Evaluated dispensing accuracy, consistency, and reliability.
  • Investigated cybersecurity vulnerabilities in the control architecture.
  • Implemented access-control protections against unauthorized manipulation.
Research contribution: The project demonstrated that embedded healthcare devices must be evaluated for both functional performance and security.

Presentation: Nebraska Conference for Undergraduate Women in Mathematics.
