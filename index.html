My Journey: From Chipinge to Code

I was born in Chipinge, Mashiri, a rural district in Manicaland, Zimbabwe, where access to quality education and technology was limited. I walked to school barefoot and often studied without basic supplies like textbooks or notebooks. Yet, through all the challenges, my grandparents instilled in me a powerful belief: education and perseverance can change lives.

Even with overcrowded classrooms and minimal resources, I stayed focused. I studied by the dim light of a kerosene lamp, driven by the dream that one day, I could harness technology to create meaningful change— not just for myself, but for others in my community.

Today, I’m pursuing a double major degree in Mathematics and Computer Science at KSC in the United States. This opportunity has expanded my perspective and strengthened my commitment to becoming a software engineer who builds innovative, inclusive, and human-centered solutions.

My passion lies in solving real-world problems through code—particularly those affecting global industries and large-scale systems. I’m currently developing AI-powered and web-based tools that simplify language learning, generate summaries, and analyze sentiment, all under my own brand, Mashasha AI.

Outside of academics, I lead as President of several student organizations, where I advocate for diversity, equity, and inclusion. These roles allow me to connect with people from all backgrounds, share my story, and empower others to pursue their goals—no matter where they start.

My journey from rural Zimbabwe to the U.S. is more than a change in geography—it’s a transformation of purpose. I carry with me the hopes of my community and a vision to build software that matters. My dream is to become a full-stack developer and a tech leader, building bridges through code and innovation—especially for those who are often overlooked.
