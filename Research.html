Medical AI & Healthcare IoT

Integrity-Aware Wound Classification Under Image Degradation

Undergraduate Research Assistant, NH-INBRE
Keene State College | Faculty Mentor: Dr. Wei Lu
May 2026–Present

This research investigates how image degradation affects the reliability of machine-learning models used for wound classification. The project evaluates model performance under blur, Gaussian noise, JPEG compression, brightness loss, reduced resolution, and other image-quality conditions.

  • Developed controlled image-degradation and preprocessing pipelines.
  • Evaluated classification accuracy, confidence, robustness, and failure modes.
  • Analyzed model behavior under realistic healthcare-IoT conditions.
  • Contributed to experimental design, analysis, and manuscript preparation.

Manuscript: “Integrity-Aware Wound Classification Under Image Degradation in Healthcare IoT Systems,” submitted to WIDECOM 2026.

AI, Automation & Quantitative Modeling

A Task-Based Quantitative Model for Predicting Occupational Exposure to Artificial Intelligence

Undergraduate Researcher
Keene State College | Faculty Mentor: Dr. Wei Lu
March 2026–Present

This project develops a task-based model for estimating how occupations may be affected by artificial intelligence and automation. The model examines individual job tasks rather than treating each occupation as fully automatable or non-automatable.

  • Cleaned and integrated occupational and task-level datasets.
  • Selected predictive variables related to technology and job characteristics.
  • Evaluated occupational exposure patterns across job categories.
  • Co-authored and presented the resulting research paper.

Presentation: International Conference on Complex, Intelligent, and Software Intensive Systems, CISIS 2026.
